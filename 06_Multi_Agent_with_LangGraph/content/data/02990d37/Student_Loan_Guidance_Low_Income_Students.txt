### Overview of Student Loans for Low-Income Students

1. **Types of Loans Available**: 
   - **Subsidized Loans**: These loans are awarded based on financial need, making them a beneficial option for low-income students. The government pays the interest on these loans while you are enrolled at least half-time in school, which helps reduce your overall debt load upon graduation.
   - **Unsubsidized Loans**: Unlike subsidized loans, these are available to all students regardless of financial need. Interest accrues from the time the loan is disbursed, which can lead to a larger debt burden over time.

2. **FAFSA and Access to Aid**: 
   - Completing the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) is crucial as it opens the door to various financial aid options, including federal loans and grants. Importantly, there is no strict income limit to qualify for federal student loans, although certain grants may consider your income level.

3. **Income-Based Repayment Plans**: 
   - For those who find themselves in a challenging financial situation post-graduation, income-based repayment plans are available. These plans cap monthly payments based on your income and family size, ensuring that repayments remain manageable and reflect your financial realities. Awareness of these options is crucial, as many borrowers with lower incomes may not fully understand them.

4. **Current Updates**: 
   - As of September 1, 2023, the 0% interest rate implemented during the COVID-19 pandemic has ended, and payments have resumed. Additionally, the government has introduced the SAVE plan, which could significantly reduce monthly payments for borrowers with undergraduate loans based on their income.
